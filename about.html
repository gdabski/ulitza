ulitza street names

Have you ever wondered what the most popular street names in your country are? Who were all those men and women you see everywhere on street signs? Who does your country consider to be its most famous heroes, leaders, poets or artists of the past that have had a long lasting influence on the public imagination? Or of whatever other country you might have an interest in? Great! So did I!

Method

All street data is taken from openstreetmap (osm). I've parsed it, cleaned it up, sorted it and I've added wikipedia links for all such entries that are named after a person (eponyms, from now on).

Since the osm entries are not always consistent, there is no clear separation between names of streets, bridges, squares, public buildings, etc. All such landmarks are taken into consideration, as long as they are eponyms. So "street names" is a bit of a useful lie.

Names of legendary figures, fictional characters (books, plays), or tribes have also sometimes been included. To avoid duplicate entries, there is only one name allowed per city. Affixes like "street", "boulevard", "square", etc. are removed. If more than one name refers to the same person, they are considered equivalent and are not counted more than once.

Page structure

The front page lists the most famous persons worldwide. That is, if a person appears at least once in a given country, they receive +1. For the country pages, the count refers to the number of cities where that name appears at least once. In both cases, clicking on the count reveals the countries where that persons appears in. The wikipedia articles and the persons names are all in English or in the country's own language, as an alternative.

Contribute

I've had some trouble with arabic text, mostly in the way I'm arranging the equivalent names due to right-to-left writing direction. Parsing and figuring out eponyms for China, Korea and Japan are complete misteries so none of them are currently included. Help!

Check out the github page for every country (click on the country name) and add, remove or adjust the wikipedia link.

Etymology

ulitza, a non-standard way of writing uliță, without using any special characters, a Romanian word meaning a narrow road, used most frequently for streets in small villages borderd on both sides by houses, in contradiction to the city boulevards, for example. Originally a proto-slavic word, ulita, it has many variants in most of the Eastern-Europeean countries and languages,

Aromanian - ulitsã
Belarus - вуліца (vúlica)
Bosnian - ulica
Bulgarian - у́лица (úlica)
Croatia - ulica
Czech - ulice, ulicě
Hungarian - utca
Romanian - uliță
Polish - ulica
Slovak - ulica
Russian - у́лица (úlica)
Slovene - úlica
Ukrainian - у́лиця (úlycja), ву́лиця (vúlycja)
